Accuracy Assessment of a Land Cover Classification

Infrastructure pieces like roads, buildings, and other structures absorb. Urban regions, which have less vegetation, experience "islands" of hotter weather than rural ones. urban heat island effect, increasing urban population development, and an increase in the frequency and length of heat waves as a result of climate change.

Urban heat islands (UHI) can be tracked over time using global, timely, objective observations provided by remote sensing. While optical data gathered by satellites can provide information on where and when land usage occurs, thermal mapping from satellites can be used to monitor land surface temperature (LST). After UHIs have been identified, heat vulnerability indices (HVI) can be used to control health risks associated with heat by combining socioeconomic data about the population and health information.
