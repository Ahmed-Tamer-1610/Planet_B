Clasificación de Cultivos Agrícolas con Radar de Apertura Sintética y Teledetección Óptica

The UN Biodiversity Lab (UNBL), which receives funding from NASA, will use remote sensing and geospatial data to address national conservation and sustainable development agendas. Without any prior knowledge of remote sensing or GIS, decision-makers and policymakers can access the most important global data, upload national datasets, and calculate dynamic indicators for any area of interest using UNBL's more than 400 of the best global data layers on biodiversity, ecosystem services, and sustainable development. UNBL will play a significant role in assisting parties and other stakeholders to implement, monitor, and report on the CBD's Post-2020 Global Biodiversity Framework when it is fully revamped in 2021 to improve functionality and better fulfil user needs (GBF).

Participants can choose from three complementing courses in this training—an intermediate lecture series (Parts 1-3) and two advanced labs—to tailor their learning to their own needs.

It will be possible to take this programme in English, Spanish, or French. Each of the three courses is available in any combination for participants.
