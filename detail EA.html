A Q&A Session on Radar Remote Sensing

Humans depend on healthy ecosystems for a variety of necessities including food, clean water, and other aspects of daily living. Natural capital accounting acknowledges the need to monitor and preserve these resources for important decision-making processes. Ecosystems may be precisely mapped using NASA data to help efficient planning and environmentally responsible decision-making.

The fundamentals of ecosystem services and natural capital accounting will be covered in this training. Additionally, it will give a general overview of the ways in which Earth Observations (EO) can support international frameworks and projects like the criteria established by the United Nations System of Environmental Economic Accounting. Participants will learn about methods for utilising EO in natural capital accounting, including the utilisation of land cover mapping and modelling initiatives carried out with the Natural Capital Project via the InVEST programme. Case-study examples will be given to highlight regionally relevant initiatives, such as accounting for urban ecosystems and valuing native pollinators.
